Fundamentals of Economics

The subject of the study of economics is the regularities that arise between the participants in the economic system in the process of their economic activity. Economic analysis is carried out at one of three levels: microeconomic, macroeconomic and global.Microeconomics studies economic activity within a single enterprise, industry or local market. Macroeconomics covers the market of any state or region. The world economy studies the influence of national markets on each other, as well as the pace and trends in the development of economic activity on a global scale.

Structure of the economy

In economic analysis, in order to obtain the most accurate conclusions, many factors must be taken into account, the degree of influence of each of which cannot be precisely determined. That is why economics is not an exact science. On the other hand, it cannot be called completely humanitarian either, since mathematical calculations are used in the construction of economic models. The structural elements of the economy that are subject to analysis are.

–

Resources, which are sources for the production of goods and services. The main postulate of economics is the fact that any resources are limited, so the needs of society cannot be fully satisfied.

–

Benefits are those phenomena and objects that society needs.

–

Production is the process of creating wealth from resources.

–

Money is an intermediary element through which goods are acquired and exchanged.

–

Capital is the accumulated surplus of goods, which are the sources for the formation of new goods.

–

A market is an abstract or concrete place where goods are exchanged between participants in an economic system.

Basic economic concepts

The fundamental concepts of economics are supply and demand. Demand means the willingness of a certain number of consumers to purchase a particular product or service at a specified cost. First of all, it depends, of course, on the price. However, a number of other factors can also have a significant impact on the volume of demand. These factors are.

–

Consumer preferences

–

Income level

–

Promotions

The offer, in turn, shows the volume of goods that, depending on a particular market price, entrepreneurs are ready to produce. Supply and demand graphs are curves whose intersection point means the equilibrium price, that is, the market value of a good or service that satisfies both consumers and sellers.

Market Principles

The main stimulus for the development of market relations is competition. Thanks to it, consumers get the opportunity to choose the necessary product at the price that suits them. The absence of a healthy competitive environment can lead to the emergence of oligopolies and monopolies that have an advantageous position in front of buyers, thanks to which they can dictate their terms.

Practice shows that competition is not always formed and develops in a natural way. Sooner or later, there may be collusion between manufacturers or sellers, because of which consumers will have to overpay for some products or services, which may also be of inadequate quality. In this regard, in some cases, it is necessary for the regulator to intervene in certain economic processes. As a rule, the state acts as such a regulator.

The degree of influence of state bodies on the domestic economy can be different. If there is a significant influence on economic activity within the country by the government, then such an economy is called a command-administrative economy. If the impact is insignificant, then the economy is considered mixed. In its pure form, a market economy within the state is not currently found.

There is also a traditional economy based on subsistence and customs. This type of economic activity is typical for various tribes that have survived to this day and live apart.
